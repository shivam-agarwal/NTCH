Tutorial of NodeTrix-Multiplex


Q) What is NodeTrix-Multiplex?

Ans. NodeTrix-Multiplex is a visual analytic tool to explore multilayer networks.


Q) What is focus+context feature of NodeTrix-Multiplex?

Ans. In NodeTrix-Multiplex, each individual matrix is called a focus. The focus is a group of nodes which together form a community. So each focus is a community.

All the matrices together (the entire network displayed) are considered as context.


Q) How do we explore using focus+context feature?

Ans. Context operations will be applicable on all the matrices (which are not frozen denoted by a lock symbol on matrices). Context operations can be viewed by clicking “Context Operations” button on top left part of the user interface. Context operations menu appears from left side of the interface. To hide context operations menu, click on the “Context Operations” button again. Watch the following video to get the idea:



Focus operations are applicable on only the selected matrix. To access the focus operations, one can right click on any matrix. A menu will appear from right side of the screen as shown in the fig 1. A green dot signifies which matrix is selected as current focus. To hide focus operations menu, left click on empty area, or click on right arrow of the appeared menu.


Figure 1: Focus operations menu.



Q) What all other interactive operations are present in the interface?

Ans. Zooming is enabled to navigate the network. Wheel up/down controls the zoom level. Additionally double left click also allows users to zoom in.

Panning is also possible if a user clicks on empty area (not on any matrix) and drags.

Individual matrices can also be dragged. The links are updated as a matrix is dragged.

Hovering on matrix will highlight the i, j th names of hovered matrix's i,j th location. Also the links/edges appearing from i th or j th location will be highlighted with a green color.
